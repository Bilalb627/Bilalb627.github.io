Volunteer Activity Coordination System (VACS) | IS Project Management

Description:

As part of a collaborative group project in the Information Systems (IS) Project Management course, I helped create and execute the Volunteer Activity Coordination System (VACS), which aims to improve volunteer management procedures for non-profit organizations. Played an important part in several elements of the project, including requirements gathering, system design, project management, and stakeholder communication.

Key Contributions:

  • Requirement Gathering and Analysis: Actively engaged in stakeholder interviews and group discussions to collect and assess needs. Collaborated with teammates to obtain a thorough grasp of user needs and preferences.

  • System Design and Development: Worked directly with team members to help design and develop the VACS platform. Utilized coursework and self-directed study skills to offer unique ideas and solve technical issues.

  • Project Planning and Execution: Assisted with the development of project plans and timeframes, as well as job allocation and scheduling. Adopted Agile approaches to react to changing needs and keep the project moving forward.

  • Stakeholder Engagement: Communicated with project stakeholders, such as classmates and instructors, to gain input and ensure alignment with project objectives. Effectively engaged in group meetings and presentations to discuss progress and gather feedback.

  • Quality Assurance and Testing: Contributed to testing and quality assurance efforts by thoroughly testing system functionality. Collaborated with teammates to identify and fix problems and usability concerns.

  • Documentation and Presentation: Helped create project documentation, such as user manuals and technical specifications. Participated in group presentations to highlight project outcomes and discuss critical takeaways with colleagues.

Outcome:

The Volunteer Activity Coordination System (VACS) project offered practical experience in project management and software development in a collaborative group environment. Our crew was successful in delivering a workable system prototype that proved the potential to expedite volunteer management operations for non-profit organizations thanks to good collaboration and communication.
